## Classical Mechanics
- Newton's First Law (Inertia) states that an object will remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force | Newton's Laws | 0.96
- Newton's Second Law defines force as the product of mass and acceleration (F = ma) | Newton's Laws | 0.96
- Newton's Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction | Newton's Laws | 0.96
- Work is defined as the product of force and displacement in the direction of the force (W = F·d) | Mechanics | 0.95
- Kinetic Energy is the energy of motion, calculated as KE = 1/2 mv² | Energy | 0.95
- Potential Energy is stored energy based on position, such as gravitational potential energy (PE = mgh) | Energy | 0.95

## Fluid Dynamics
- Pressure in a fluid is the force exerted per unit area, often measured in Pascals (Pa) | Fluid Mechanics | 0.95
- Pascal's Principle states that pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished to every portion of the fluid | Fluid Mechanics | 0.93
- Bernoulli's Principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid occurs simultaneously with a decrease in pressure or potential energy | Fluid Mechanics | 0.92
- Viscosity is a measure of a fluid's resistance to deformation or flow | Fluid Properties | 0.94
- Laminar flow is characterized by smooth, parallel layers of fluid; turbulent flow is characterized by chaotic changes in pressure and velocity | Flow Types | 0.93
- Surface tension is the cohesive force at the surface of a liquid that causes it to behave like a stretched elastic membrane | Fluid Properties | 0.92

## Waves and Electromagnetism
- A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy through matter or space without transferring mass | Wave Theory | 0.95
- Frequency (f) is the number of wave cycles that pass a point per unit of time, measured in Hertz (Hz) | Waves | 0.95
- The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 3.0 × 10^8 meters per second | Optics | 0.97
- Ohm's Law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points (V = IR) | Electricity | 0.96
- Electromagnetic waves consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields and can travel through a vacuum | Electromagnetism | 0.95
